8) What metaphor or metaphors does the experience's mental model reference?

The website utilises the direct manipulation of everyday life. While moving the cursor the audio/visual elements change.

The visual element mimics that of water, the feeling of a finger running through the water. I think this is an appealing design as water is embedded in our biology and instinctive to our survival, so when seeing water movement it can naturally please us. to add to this, when the mouse stops the visual becomes still again, much like how water will naturall become stagnic when undisturbed.

These strokes are also reminicemnt of painting and brush strokes. However unlike a painting, the visual reverts to the original, thus always having a blank canvas that the use can play with.

The audio visual/visual experience mimcs the interplay of visual and audio in everyday life and of instruments. Furthermore, the descriptors of the control options are applicaple to both the audio and visual elements; blurring the line of the descirptin of textures (e.g lightness, reverb, gleam).

The shininess and gleam gave the page a sense of magic and childlike wonder.

The slide functions feel skeumorphic of the controls to sound and video mixers and controls/
